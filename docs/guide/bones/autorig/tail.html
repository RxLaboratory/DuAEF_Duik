Tails and hair

In Duik, tails, hair, and anything hanged on a character like a scarf or cloth can be rigged using a tail or hair armature.

The only difference between hair and tail rigs is that a hair rig doesn’t inherit the rotation from the limb it’s attached to; that helps keeping the hair (or cloth, or scarf…) hanging vertically even if the head or body rotates.


A tail rigged using FK controls

By default, hair and tails are rigged using FK controls; that means they’re controlled by a single controller at the root of the limb, which can be animated in rotation, with a lot of options in the effects of the controller1.

Although most of the time it’s muc easier to animate these limbs with FK, you may need IK instead in very specific cases (e.g. when the dog’s tail gets caught in the door. Ouch!). In this case, you can switch the Long Chain rig option in the settings of the auto-rig2.

This way the tail can be rigged using Bézier IK or Bézier FK. In this case, three controllers are added: the main controller at the tip of the tail to be animated in position, and two other controllers to have more control on the curvature of the tail1.


A tail rigged using Bézier IK controls

  2. Read the Bones and Auto-Rig / Autorig / Introduction section for more information about the options of the Auto-rig. ↩
